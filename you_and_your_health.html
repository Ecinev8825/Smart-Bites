Why your health matters

Your health is important because it lets you enjoy life more. For example, you can’t play your favorite sport if you’re sick. Eating healthy foods helps keep you strong and energetic, so you don’t miss out on important or exciting events.

What other things are important

Besides eating well, getting enough sleep and exercise is important too. Eating healthy won’t help much if you’re always tired or sleepy. Exercise keeps your body strong and helps your health even more.

Why you matter

Your health isn’t just about eating well, exercising, or getting enough sleep. You matter too. After all, those habits wouldn’t mean much if you weren’t here to actually do them.


Many people struggle with unhealthy eating habits, and the cause isn’t always physical—it can also be mental. Stress, anxiety, and other challenges can affect the choices we make every day.


When your mental health improves, healthier habits often follow. Taking care of your mind is just as important as taking care of your body.


School can get overwhelming—assignments, projects, presentations, and groupmates who mysteriously disappear when it’s time to work. When that happens, it’s okay to pause for a moment. Take a short break. Grab a snack—maybe something healthy like an apple. Your brain is working hard, and it deserves some fuel.


Grades are important, but your well-being matters more. Learning should help you grow, not break you.


Remember: you matter more than any deadline or grade. And even if it doesn’t always feel like it, there are people who care about you—some are just quiet about it.
